pH 6–7 ideal for most crops.

Acidic Soil Treatment:
- Apply lime.
- Increase organic matter.

Alkaline Soil Treatment:
- Gypsum application.
- Use compost.

Nitrogen Deficiency:
- Yellowing from bottom leaves.

Phosphorus Deficiency:
- Purpling of leaves.

Potassium Deficiency:
- Leaf edge burning.

Micronutrient Deficiencies:
- Zinc: Shortened leaves
- Iron: Yellow young leaves

Soil Testing:
- Test every 2 years via Soil Health Card.

Organic Carbon:
- Ideal >0.75% for fertility.
# Soil pH, NPK, and Soil Management
# Source: Soil Health Card, ICAR Institutes

Soil pH requirements:
- Rice: 5.0–6.5
- Wheat: 6.0–7.5
- Potato: 5.0–6.0
- Maize: 5.5–7.5

Improving Acidic Soils:
- Apply lime (dose based on SHC)
- Add organic matter

Improving Alkaline Soils:
- Apply gypsum
- Use compost, green manuring

Nutrient Deficiency Indicators:
- Nitrogen: Yellowing from bottom leaves
- Phosphorus: Purpling of leaves
- Potassium: Leaf edge burning

Soil Testing:
- Use SHC every 2 years
- Adjust fertilizer based on nutrient status
